Quick Glossary — what do these words mean?

📈
Active Realtor
A realtor who closed at least one valid sale within the selected time window (e.g. last 2 years). The same person is counted in every window that includes their most recent closing.
🚫
Potentially Inactive
A realtor who had past sales but has not closed any transaction within the threshold period (1, 1.5, or 2 years). They may have left the industry.
📋
Historical Transactions
The total count of all valid sales (ever, across all years) where the realtor appeared as buyer agent, co-buyer agent, listing agent, or co-listing agent.
📋
Valid Sale
A sale record where deleted = false AND duplicate = false. These are the only records counted in all statistics on this page.
📈
Business Trend
Compares a realtor's last-12-month transactions to their historical annual average. Only realtors active in the last year with at least one prior year of history are included. Buckets show the population distribution at ±15%, ±30%, ±50%, and ±80% change thresholds.
🏠
Homeowner Without Agent
A property whose most recent valid sale was handled exclusively by realtors now classified as inactive. No newer transaction by any active agent has occurred on this property — the homeowner is currently unrepresented in the market.
🔑
Buyer-Side Homeowner Without Agent
A property whose buyer was represented by a realtor who has since gone inactive. The homeowner was helped to buy by a now-inactive agent and — if no active agent appears on their most recent sale record — currently has no representation to help them sell.

How to read this block: These are the same inactive realtors from Block C, but here we count only the transactions where they appeared as buyer_agent or buyer_co_agent — the buyer’s side of the deal. This matters because realtors typically begin their careers helping clients buy a home before transitioning to listing properties. Capturing buyer-side activity therefore reveals a fuller historical footprint for agents who may later have shifted focus or left the market.

Inactivity definition is identical to Blocks B and C: last_sale_date is derived from all 4 agent roles combined, so the inactivity threshold is the same — only what is counted changes.

Two denominators are shown for transparency:

  • Secondary % (row note) = buyer-role transactions ÷ total valid sales in DB — keeps figures comparable with Block C percentages.

Block C3H — Homeowners Without Active Buyer Agent

How to read this block: Each number is a count of unique properties (one per property_id) whose most recent buyer-agent has been inactive for longer than the stated threshold. These represent homeowners who purchased their home through a buyer’s agent that has since left the active market — and who have not had any subsequent transaction recorded (see lost_deal = false below).

Buyer-only roles: Only buyer_agent_id and buyer_co_agent_id columns are considered. Listing-side agents are excluded because this block focuses on the purchase relationship between a homeowner and the agent who helped them buy.

lost_deal = false filter: Sales flagged as lost_deal = true indicate that a subsequent transaction already exists for that property — meaning the homeowner has re-engaged the market through another agent or sale. These properties are excluded so that only genuinely unrepresented homeowners are counted.

Deduplication: Each property_id is counted once per threshold, regardless of how many buyer-agent sales are on record for that property. A property is included in the inactive pool if any of its buyer-agents has been inactive since the cutoff date.

% base: All percentages are calculated as homeowner count ÷ total unique properties × 100. The denominator is 32,408,743 distinct property_id values across all valid transactions.

Block E — Business Trend Analysis

How % change is calculated: for each qualifying realtor we compute avg_annual_tx = total_tx ÷ years_active (where years_active = year(as-of) − year(first sale), minimum 1), then pct_change = (tx_last_1yr − avg_annual_tx) ÷ avg_annual_tx × 100. Example: a realtor with 250 total transactions over 5 years has avg = 50 transactions/yr. If they closed 42 deals in the last year: (42 − 50) ÷ 50 × 100 = −16% → they appear in Declining > −15%.

Rows are cumulative — each threshold count includes all realtors who crossed it, regardless of how far beyond it they went. For example, a realtor with +90% growth is counted in all four growth rows (>+15%, >+30%, >+50%, >+80%). Counts therefore always decrease as the threshold gets stricter: Growing > +15% ≥ Growing > +30% ≥ Growing > +50% ≥ Growing > +80%. Only the Stable band is non-overlapping with the growth/decline rows.

Only realtors active in the last year AND with at least one prior year of history are included, ensuring the comparison is meaningful.
